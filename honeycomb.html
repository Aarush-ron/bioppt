How Shapes
Save Lives

An interactive crash simulator that shows why nature's honeycomb design is the best at absorbing energy and protecting passengers during high-speed impacts.

⬡

Nature's Geometry

The 120-degree internal angles of a hexagon naturally prevent localized stress. This makes it structurally superior and far more stable than 90-degree square grids under extreme force.

📉

Safe Energy Plateau

Instead of violently snapping, the honeycomb walls fold smoothly like an accordion. This creates a steady deceleration curve, keeping forces safely managed for the passenger.

🚀

Maximum Stopping Power

Honeycomb provides the highest specific energy absorption known in modern engineering. It absorbs the maximum kinetic impact while keeping the vehicle extremely lightweight.

🏎️

Global NCAP 64 km/h Standard

The globally accepted threshold for frontal crash testing is 64 km/h (40 mph). At this speed, kinetic energy multiplies dangerously, requiring advanced structural buckling to ensure passenger survival.
